02 — Permit Requirements
⚠️ Three separate permits are required for illuminated signs
  1. Zoning Permit — Verifies sign complies with Denver Zoning Code. Apply via e-permits → select "Building Log" for simultaneous review.
  2. Building Permit — Required for structural attachment. Structural plans must be prepared, stamped, and signed by a Colorado-registered professional structural engineer.
  3. Electrical Permit — Required for all signs with electrical components. Apply separately via e-permits → Quick Permits track.
New business tenants: if prior tenant was a different use type, additional zoning use permit documentation is required. Confirm with CPD before submitting.
03 — Key Dimensional Rules
Max Sign Area
1.5 sq ft / LF
Of building frontage, up to 200 sq ft max. Verify exact cap for your zone district (C-MX zones may vary).
Max Projection
12 inches
From building face. Includes raceway + letter depth combined.
Max Height
Roofline / Parapet
Sign top cannot exceed roofline or parapet. Measure to top of letters, not raceway bottom.
Signs Per Frontage
1 Primary
One wall sign per street frontage typical. Secondary ID/logo signs may be exempt — confirm with CPD.

04 — Illumination Rules
✅ Permitted
LED channel letters (front-lit / face-illuminated)
Externally illuminated signs (shielded light source)
Neon signs
EMC / electronic message centers (min. 8-second hold; brightness limits apply)
❌ Not Permitted
Animated or flashing illumination
EMC with rapid cycling (< 8-second hold)
Unshielded external light sources directed off-property
05 — Overlay Districts to Verify
⚠️ These neighborhoods have design review boards — additional approval required
  • LoDo (Lower Downtown Historic District)
  • Baker Historic District
  • Curtis Park / Five Points
  • Ballpark Neighborhood
  • Golden Triangle Creative District
Colfax Ave (east of downtown) is primarily C-MX zone and typically NOT in a historic overlay — but always confirm against the specific parcel using Denver's zoning map before submitting. If in an overlay, Landmark Preservation must issue a Certificate of Appropriateness before the sign permit is processed.
